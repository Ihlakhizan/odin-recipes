Rajma Masala

Description

A delicately spiced, wonderfully creamy Punjabi-style curry made with kidney beans, tomatoes, and onions. Goes wonderfully with steamed rice or rotis.

Fairly straightforward recipe, too! Doesn't take too long to make and isn't difficult.

Ingredients

  • 200 grams rajma
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 2 green chillies
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 large tomatoes
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak the kidney beans in enough water for 8 hours.
  2. Discard the water and rinse the beans in fresh water.
  3. Drain the water.
  4. Pressure cook the beans for 15-20 minutes on a high flame.
  5. Crush the ginger, garlic, and green chillies to a fine paste.
  6. Heat butter in a pot on a low heat.
  7. Add cumin seeds and let them crackle and brown.
  8. Add onions and saute them until golden brown.
  9. Add the ginger-garlic-chilli paste. Stir for 10 seconds on a low flame.
  10. Add the tomatoes. Stir for 2 minutes.
  11. Add all the spice powders.
  12. Keep sauteing until the fat starts leaving the sides of the masala base.
  13. Add the beans to the masala. Saute for 1 minute.
  14. Add 2 cups of water and salt as required.
  15. Simmer for 10-12 minutes until the curry thickens.
  16. Add the kasuri methi.
  17. Switch off the heat and add coriander leaves.
